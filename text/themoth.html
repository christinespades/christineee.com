T H E M O T H

          the moth

    already unraveling—
         the slow insistence
               of change.

- I -
i felt the weight of your first transgressive glance pressing against my ribs.
it could never withdraw its grasp.
the shelter of this moment shattered into irretrievable dust.

we were a failing organ, a fused unit, an organism slowly dying.
a pulse was quickening beneath the skin of our shared life.
a quiet theft was pulling you further into a world i could not reach.
already softening into something nocturnal,
already unraveling—
the slow insistence of change.

not merciful, not sudden,
but a patient undoing of form,
a dissolving into instinct and ache.

to not recognize the flame,
only to choose it.
in the space between the two, i lived.

-

i carried her. as sorrow. as devotion.
as the ache that reminded me i had once felt something so vast, it eclipsed everything else.
he sometimes opened old voicemails just to hear her tone again, though he never pressed play.
he still remembered the way she held her cup with both hands.

what was the purpose of outliving you
if survival only meant this:
a dim corridor of unfinished hours,
a body continuing out of habit,
your name settling into me
like weather into stone.

i do not think you understood
how much of the world departed with you.
not love—
something prior to it.
the quiet architecture of tomorrow.

now everything repeats itself imperfectly.
the same rooms,
the same evenings collapsing at the same angle.
memory turning slowly beneath the surface
like something submerged that never found the strength to drown.

i have become a place abandoned carefully.
dust gathering without interruption.
a lantern left burning in an empty house.
pages missing from the final chapter.

still,
go on living.
i ask this of you without bitterness.
it is enough to imagine you somewhere beneath another season,
your hands warmed by ordinary light,
your thoughts brushing mine by accident
once,
perhaps,
in some unguarded hour.

as for me,
i will remain where the leaving placed me.
not waiting.
not recovering.
only continuing
in the shape you last recognized.
