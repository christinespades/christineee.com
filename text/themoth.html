T H E M O T H

- I -
i felt the weight of your first transgressive glance pressing against my ribs.
it could never withdraw its grasp.
the shelter of this moment shattered into irretrievable dust.

we were a failing organ, a fused unit, an organism slowly dying.
a pulse was quickening beneath the skin of our shared life.
a quiet theft was pulling you further into a world i could not reach.
already softening into something nocturnal,
already unraveling—
the slow insistence of change.

not merciful, not sudden,
but a patient undoing of form,
a dissolving into instinct and ache.

to not recognize the flame,
only to choose it.
in the space between the two, i lived.

-

i carried her. as sorrow. as devotion.
as the ache that reminded me i had once felt something so vast, it eclipsed everything else.
he sometimes opened old voicemails just to hear her tone again, though he never pressed play.
he still remembered the way she held her cup with both hands.
