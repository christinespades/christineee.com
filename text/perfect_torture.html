in every life, the arrival of noontide is scheduled.
then is the time in which we cast no shadow.
nothing lingers behind us; no past gives shape, no tradition casts relief.
we are exposed to the flat, unyielding glare of existence, and all values, all meaning, collapse under its heat.
to cast no shadow is to confront the void: every principle, every aspiration, is revealed as contingent, mutable, ephemeral.
there is nothing to hide in, nothing to clutch for comfort.
and yet, in this exposure, the mind seizes a terrible freedom: the freedom to abandon, to invent, or to endure the glaring abyss of its own making.
sauve qui peut.
